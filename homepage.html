What is Cyberstalking?

According to Wikipedia, Cyberstalking is a technologically-based "attack" on one person who has been targeted specifically for that attack for reasons of anger, revenge or control. Cyberstalking can take many forms, including: harassment, embarrassment and humiliation of the victim. Cyberstalking can cause a wide range of physical and emotional consequences for those who are targeted.

Examples of Cyberstalking

  • Hacking into or hijacking the target's online accounts
  • Releasing confidential information online
  • Tracking the target's online movements by installing tracking devices
  • Posting or distributing real or fake photos of the target
  • Hacking into the target's camera on their laptop or smartphone as a way to secretly record them
  • Attempting to extort sex or explicit photos

How do we prevent Cyberstalking?

  1. Make sure you have strong passwords for all your online accounts as well as strong passwords for your devices and remember to set a reminder on your phone to regularly change your passwords.
  2. Use caution on public wifi by refraining from using public wifi or invest in VPN.
  3. Practice online safety habits, make it a priority to only accept friend requests from people you know and keep your posts private.
  4. Leave the optional sections, like your date of birth or your hometown, blank.
  5. Consider disabling the geolocation settings in photos, also refrain from posting your location in real time and instead post photos showing where you have been after the fact.

How do we deal with Online Stalkers?

  1. Respond only once to the person cyberstalking you, tell them to stop contacting you and make sure you block the person cyberstalking you from all your accounts.
  2. Consider suspending online accounts. If you are not active online, it's harder for the person cyberstalking you to reach you.
  3. You should have your devices checked out by a professional to ensure the person harassing you did not install tracking devices or hack your computer in some way.
  4. Keep copies of everything the person cyberstalking you has sent. Make a copy for yourself and a copy for law enforcement.
  5. Lastly, please notify your local police and file an official complaint if you're being cyberstalked. Even if they cannot do anything right away, having an official complaint on file is important if the behavior persists or escalates.
